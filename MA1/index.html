Artists

The emergence of graphic art deco is best represented by French poster artist Georges Lepape. Evoking the art nouveau movement, Lepape transformed book covers, magazines, playbills, and more. In fact, he designed some of the most iconic cover art for Vogue and Vanity Fair.

As the Art Deco movement morphed in the 1920s, French artists who define the period include Paul Colin and Jean Carlu. The former created renowned posters for Josephine Baker, an American Entertainer.

Carlu's influence dominated in theatre, soaps, and movie posters, including Charlie Chaplin. In the 1930s and 40s, his focus in design, along with most other Art Deco artists, shifted towards the war and promoting tourism.

Designs

Although the Art Deco period had a wide influence in many different forms, including architecture and art, some of the most influential uses of this were in the graphic arts. Art Deco appeared early on in the development of posters and magazines, therefore creating some of the most renowned covers and promotional tourism.

One such poster is L'Atlantique by artist A.M. Cassandre. This poster embodies the elegance and grandeur that the Art Deco movement emphasized when advertising travels. The bold, geometric wall representing the ship illustrates it as nearly a wall trasversing through the Atlantic.

History

The Art Deco movement's influence in architecture, fashion, and design can still be seen today. Many iconic buildings from the time are still standing and demand the viewer's attention in their grandiosity. However, the graphic arts of the time are particularly interesting as their shift in focus and design are particularly connected to our history.

The first Art Deco graphic art can be seen leading up to World War I. The posters focused on the lavish elegance of ballets and fashion of the time. Then, in the 1920's, as fashion evolved to become more casual and daring, so too did the posters; fashion magazines featuring this type of aesthetic quickly popularized the style in America.

During the Great Depression in the 1930s, designs shifted focus to promote tourism and cultural events. Many artists, including Jean Carlu, also shifted their focus on war efforts and patriotic posters during the second World War.
